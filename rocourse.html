  • Robust combinatorial optimization
    Proposed for the academic year 2025-2026
  • Instructors: Ayse N. Arslan and Michael Poss.
  • Prerequisits: Linear algebra and basic programming skills.
  • Scope:
    • Mixed-integer programming has emerged as a very efficient technology to model and solve optimization problems that arise in many decision-making contexts (transportation and logistics, healthcare, policy-making, etc.). State-of-the-art solution approaches such as the well-known branch-and-bound algorithm assume that the problem parameters (such as costs, availability of resources) are known (with certainty) in advance. Yet, in practice, many of these problems are plagued with uncertainty on some of these parameters, be it because of measurement errors, lack of complete information or the long-term nature of decision-making processes. Robust optimization is a framework that has been introduced in the last decades and is rapidly becoming a key methodology to account for this uncertainty within the context of optimization. Its main advantages are (i) the small amount of information required concerning the uncertain parameters, modeling their values through convex sets, and (ii) its tractability, since many robust optimization problems are not much harder to solve to optimality than their counterparts ignoring the uncertainty. This course will provide an introduction to robust (linear) optimization from the perspective of mathematical programming with a particular focus on mixed-integer programming. It will cover state-of-the-art formulations and solution algorithms as well as more cutting-edge research questions. Students completing the course will have acquired advanced methodological and algorithmic notions both in mathematical programming and robust optimization.
  • Content:
    • Introduction to basic notions of integer programming (modelling, relaxation, branch-and-bound, decomposition etc.).
    • Introduction to basic notions of robust optimization and fundamental results (duality, reformulations, separation algorithms etc.).
    • Robust combinatorial optimization, detailing techniques combining combinatorial algorithms with robust optimization.
    • Introduction to adjustable robust optimization in which some of the optimization variables can be fixed after the uncertain parameters have been revealed and associated algorithms.
  • Evaluation:
    • Mid-term: small project relying on Julia programming language using the JuMP library.
    • Final: Reading and presenting selected scientific papers.
